# Research Frameworks Reference

The proven backbone. Diagnose first (§1-2), then mine (§3-4), then classify findings (§5-6), then tear down winners (§7).

## §1 — The 5 Levels of Awareness (Eugene Schwartz)

Classify the TARGET AVATAR, not the market average. Each level dictates where the ad must start.

| Level | State | Ad must open with | Example open |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1. Unaware | Doesn't know they have the problem | Story/identity that makes the problem visible | "I thought being exhausted at 3pm was just adulthood..." |
| 2. Problem-aware | Feels the pain, doesn't know solutions exist | The pain, vividly mirrored in their words | "You know that wired-but-tired feeling that coffee makes worse?" |
| 3. Solution-aware | Knows the solution category exists | Why this mechanism beats the category | "Most calming gummies just sedate you. This works differently." |
| 4. Product-aware | Knows your product, hasn't bought | Proof, offer, objection-kill | "10,000 reviews later, here's what people actually say..." |
| 5. Most-aware | Ready, needs a reason NOW | Deal, urgency, reminder | "Back in stock. Bundle ends Sunday." |

Rule: one ad = one awareness level. A cold-traffic VSL typically enters at 2, walks the viewer to 4-5. The scriptwriter needs the entry level stated on every angle bank entry.

## §2 — The 5 Stages of Market Sophistication (Schwartz)

Classify the MARKET (how many similar claims has this avatar already heard?). This determines which claim types are dead.

| Stage | Market state | Winning move |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Virgin market, first claim wins | State the claim simply: "Lose weight" |
| 2 | Claim heard before | Enlarge it: "Lose 10lbs in 10 days" |
| 3 | Claims exhausted | Lead with a NEW MECHANISM: "the enzyme trick" |
| 4 | Mechanisms exhausted | Enlarge/refine the mechanism: "2nd-gen enzyme, works while you sleep" |
| 5 | Everything exhausted, total skepticism | Identity + experience: sell belonging, not claims (this is where brand world / mascot / movement wins — the Grüns move) |

Most supplement/wellness DTC markets are stage 4-5. Diagnosis method: collect the top 20 running ads in the category; list their claims and mechanisms; if 3+ competitors use the same mechanism, it's burnt — hunt the next mechanism or jump to identity play.

## §3 — VoC Mining Method

Sources ranked by signal quality:
1. **1-star reviews of competitors** — pain with the current solution = your angle ammunition ("it made me groggy", "tastes like chalk", "auto-ship scam").
2. **5-star reviews of competitors** — the desired end state in customer words, plus the moment of transformation ("first time I slept through the night in years").
3. **Reddit/forums** — unguarded language, failed-solution history, objections and skepticism scripts.
4. **YouTube/TikTok comments on category content** — questions = awareness gaps; arguments = sophistication signals.
5. **Q&A sections (Amazon)** — pre-purchase objections verbatim.

Extraction schema per quote: verbatim text | source URL | pain/desire/objection/identity | intensity (1-3) | awareness level it implies | avatar guess. Cluster quotes into named pain points; the biggest cluster with the least competitor coverage = priority drill target (Mode 2).

## §4 — Pain-Point Matrix Dimensions

For each pain point capture: the moment it peaks (time of day, life event), the failed solutions tried (each = an enemy for angle §5), the cost (functional/emotional/social), the identity dimension (who they'd be without it), and the dream-state sensory detail. Moment mapping matters most for hooks: "3pm crash at your desk" out-hooks "low energy" every time.

## §5 — Angle Taxonomy

An angle = the strategic argument; a hook = its first 3 seconds. Bank every angle under one primary class:

1. **New mechanism** — "the real reason X happens, and the thing that fixes it" (stage-3/4 killer)
2. **Enemy/villain** — position against a failed solution or industry practice ("Big powder", "melatonin hangovers")
3. **Identity/tribe** — buy this = be this (stage-5 play; pairs with brand world)
4. **Transformation story** — before→after arc, testimonial-led
5. **Social proof avalanche** — volume of results as the argument itself
6. **Curiosity/information-gap** — "the 5-second ritual", pattern the viewer must resolve
7. **Us-vs-category (demo)** — visible product superiority, side-by-sides
8. **Contrarian/myth-flip** — "everything you know about X is backwards"
9. **New opportunity** — not a fix but a door to a better life (Kell-compatible: hope > fear for cold scale)
10. **Fear/consequence** — cost of inaction (use sparingly; fatigues fast, compliance risk in health)

Each bank entry: class, awareness entry level, sophistication stage it works at, avatar, pain-point link, evidence (competitor longevity or our test data).

## §6 — Hook Classification

Classify every captured hook (first 3 seconds, transcribed verbatim):

1. **Callout** — names the avatar/pain directly ("If you're over 40 and tired by noon...")
2. **Pattern interrupt** — visual/verbal WTF that demands resolution
3. **Bold claim** — big promise up front (needs stage ≤3 or heavy proof)
4. **Question** — opens a loop the viewer must close
5. **Story open** — in-media-res personal narrative ("My doctor looked at my bloodwork and went quiet")
6. **Negative/anti-sell** — "Do NOT buy ashwagandha until you see this"
7. **Demonstration** — product doing the impossible in frame 1
8. **Testimonial cold-open** — real person mid-emotion
9. **Us-vs-them split** — two options on screen, one crossed out
10. **Trend-jack/native** — mimics platform-native content the avatar already watches

Also log: visual hook (what's on screen) separately from audio hook (what's said) and text hook (overlay). Winners usually stack all three.

## §7 — Winner Teardown → Kell Beat Map

For each long-running winner, transcribe and segment, then map to the VSL beat structure the scriptwriter uses:

| Segment | Timestamp | What they did | Beat mapping |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hook (0-3s) | | hook class + all 3 layers (visual/audio/text) | → Hook |
| Lead (3-30s) | | pain mirror / story / promise | → Lead/Big Promise |
| Body | | mechanism reveal, education | → Mechanism/Story |
| Proof | | testimonials, demos, authority | → Proof stack |
| Close | | offer construction, urgency | → Offer |
| CTA | | exact ask + friction removers | → CTA |

Then answer: WHY is this running so long? (angle freshness for the stage? avatar precision? proof density? offer?) — that "why" is the transplantable asset, and it goes in the angle bank as evidence.

## §8 — Longevity & Winner Signals

Proof hierarchy (strongest first): (1) our own test data; (2) ad active 60+ days with multiple variants (advertiser is scaling it); (3) ad active 30+ days; (4) high engagement but young — treat as hypothesis only; (5) went viral organically — weakest signal for paid. In Meta's ad library, many near-identical variants of one concept = the advertiser found a winner and is milking it; that concept is validated at their spend, free.
