# Deliverable Templates

Copy these structures for each phase output. Replace bracketed placeholders. Keep customer quotes verbatim wherever marked.

## §1 — 01-avatar-research.md

```markdown
# Avatar Research — [Brand/Product]

## Primary Avatar: "[Nickname, e.g., The Burnt-Out Optimizer]"
- Snapshot: [age range, life stage, income band, where they scroll]
- Day in the life: [3-5 sentences, concrete]
- Current alternatives: [what they buy/do now instead]
- Frustrations with alternatives: [ranked list]

### Identity Ladder (the core)
- Who they are today: [...]
- Who they're hoping to become: [...]
- What buying this says about them — to themselves: "[first-person sentence]"
- What buying this says about them — to others: "[first-person sentence]"

### Emotional Drivers (ranked)
1. [driver] — [one-line evidence]
2. ...

### Voice of Customer (verbatim quotes)
> "[real quote from review/forum]" — [source]
(minimum 5 quotes; these feed Phase 3 value-prop language)

### Objection Map
| Objection | Dissolving belief | Where addressed (ad/PDP/email) |
|---|---|---|

## Secondary Avatar: "[Nickname]" (condensed version of above)
```

## §2 — 02-category-analysis.md

```markdown
# Category Analysis — [Category]

## Positioning Map
Axes: [X-axis] × [Y-axis]
[Plot each competitor; describe the white space in 2-3 sentences]

## Category Codes
| Code | Every brand does it? | Keep (legitimacy) or Break (sameness)? | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
(visual codes: palette norms, packaging format, photography style;
verbal codes: claim patterns, tone norms)

## Category Entry Points
[The 3-6 real-life moments/needs that trigger purchase in this category, each with the mental shortlist the customer runs at that moment]

## Competitor Teardowns (one block each)
### [Competitor]
- Their platform in one sentence: [...]
- Their avatar's why: [...]
- What they own: [...] / What they've left open: [...]
```
