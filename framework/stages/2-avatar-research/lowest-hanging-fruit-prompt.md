# Lowest Hanging Fruit Prompt

Run after the Stage 1 kickoff prompt, in the same session.

---

Based on the product I listed above, help me find the low-hanging fruit buyers
who are easiest to convert along with the 1 biggest mass market buying
demographic who represents 90%+ of the size of the market by answering these
questions:

1. **CURRENT SPENDERS** — Who is already spending money trying to solve this problem?
   - What clinics/services do they visit?
   - What treatments are they paying for?
   - What's the cost they're currently paying?
   - What supplements/products are they buying?

2. **ADJACENT MARKETS** — Who is solving a RELATED problem and would naturally need my solution next?
   - What other treatments/protocols do they pair with?
   - What's the natural "stack" or combination?
   - Who's already halfway there?

3. **FRUSTRATED USERS** — Who tried the expensive/hard way and is fed up?
   - What are the complaints about current solutions?
   - What's too expensive, too inconvenient, too risky?
   - Search online for: "[competitor/treatment] worth it?"

4. **RESEARCHER STAGE** — Who is researching the "big scary solution" but can't pull the trigger?
   - What are they scared of? (cost, side effects, legality)
   - What would be the "safer/easier" stepping stone?
   - What are they Googling? "[expensive solution] alternatives"

5. **COMPARISON SHOPPERS** — Who is actively comparing options RIGHT NOW?
   - What search terms show buying intent?
   - What forums/subreddits are they asking for advice in?
   - What are the "vs" searches? "[Option A] vs [Option B]"

6. **PLATEAU POPULATION** — Who already has a solution but hit a wall?
   - What treatments stop working after a while?
   - Who needs to "stack" or add something?
   - Who's posting "X stopped working for me"?

Now rank these segments by:
- Market size (how many people)
- Buyer readiness (how close to purchase)
- Your advantage (why you're better than what they're doing)

Give me the top 3 low-hanging fruit segments to target first. Then give me
1 Avatar That Represents 10-20x More People Than The Others.

Answer each one of these questions for each avatar:

**KEY BENEFIT/PROBLEM**
- What specific kind of person (identity) in 2-4 words is this angle focused on?
- What is the main core problem that is deeply affecting their life the product ends, in 1-2 words?
- What is the 1 most important outcome they want?
- What is the main core benefit this product creates in 1-2 words?
- Who is the best kind of person they would seek advice from to solve this problem?
- What kind of person would be a world-class expert at solving this problem and would have already solved this 1,000 times for other people as well?
- What's The Big Idea our avatar would buy into that would allow them to believe 10/10 that this product will end the problem better than anything else on the market?

Write it all in words I can easily understand, and don't make up words. Search
online and use THEIR words — say it like how THEY describe their own problems.
Don't MAKE anything up.

Rank each angle by Eugene Schwartz's Market Sophistication Level and also by
market size.
