# Brand Platform — Fairy Flame (liitt)

> **Brand-architect Phase 3 — REVIEW GATE.** Built on `01-avatar-research.md` + `02-category-analysis.md`. Name is LOCKED (Fairy Flame under liitt) — naming work skipped per brief. Awaiting brand-world approval before Phases 4–6.

## Positioning Statement

For the high-functioning woman 40–55 who has done everything right and still doesn't feel like herself, **Fairy Flame** is the legal microdose gummy that gently brings *her* back — not coffee's push, not wine's numb, not a pill's flatline — because every batch carries real, pre-measured doses with a third-party lab report, on a 45-day return arc.

*(Secondary-avatar variants: for the blocked microdoser, the frame shifts to "the safe, legal on-ramp to the thing you've already researched"; for the sober ritual-seeker, "the new liquid in the glass." Same platform, different doors.)*

## Brand World

The raw material: the locked name (a fairy's flame — a small, living, magical light), the flame-shaped deep-magenta gummy, the dark indigo/gold "editorial mystic" design system with its bioluminescent accent, and the market's own after-language: *"The world is brighter. I am calmer. I find myself singing out loud."*

### ★ Recommended: **The Glow**

A state of being, not a place: **the light coming back on inside her.**

- **The physics:** Inside the Glow, she is lit from within. Colors have saturation again, jokes land, mornings have edges instead of fog. The world didn't change — her wattage did. Small magic, not fireworks: the Glow flickers on in week one, steadies by week three, holds by day 45. Everyone can see it before she says a word.
- **The door:** The nightly flame — one small gummy, the pouch-rip, the quiet minute she takes for herself. (For the sober ritual-seeker, the door IS the 8pm ritual: the new thing in the glass's place.)
- **Why this world (identity-ladder trace):** Her ladder runs *"I don't recognize her" → "I want myself back" → "I'm getting me back on my own terms."* The Glow is restoration made visible — you don't enter it by becoming someone new; it's *her own light, relit*. And critically, a glow is inherently **witnessed**: the husband notices, the kids notice, the woman at pickup asks what changed. That converts the dataset's single most powerful emotional structure (identity loss witnessed by family → comeback witnessed by family) into the brand's core mechanic. Buying Fairy Flame = turning your light back on; sharing it = "you can see it on me."
- **Performance test:** endless hook supply ("You got your glow back — what changed?", before/after wattage, the-moment-he-noticed), native platform language ("glow up") already primed, and the bioluminescent gold-on-indigo accent in the existing design system *is* the Glow rendered in tokens — zero visual retrofit.
- **Extension test:** states extend better than places — Deep Glow (sleep), Morning Glow (focus), a future kids'/partner line = new glows, same physics, new door. The existing day-60 "Glow invite" in the offer path suggests this word is already latent in the brand's own thinking.
- **One guardrail:** "glow" must always mean *inner light returning* — never skin/beauty glow. The beauty category owns that word's surface meaning; we own its restoration meaning. Copy rule: the Glow is seen in her *behavior* (singing, laughing, patience), not her complexion.

### Alternate B: **The Clearing**

A place: the quiet clearing just past the fog — where the noise stops and she can hear herself again.

- Physics: in the Clearing, the merry-go-round stops spinning; thoughts finish; the fog has an exit. Door: the first gummy is the step through the treeline.
- Identity-ladder trace: strong — "surviving in a fog" is her most literal pain language, and the Clearing is where the woman from the old photos was waiting.
- **Why not:** it's a *destination* metaphor — it frames relief as being somewhere else, when her deepest wish is to be *herself* anywhere (at dinner, in meetings, at pickup). Harder to witness from outside (the family can't see a clearing), and forest-place aesthetics drift toward the earthy/MUD\WTR world our design system deliberately isn't.

### Alternate C: **Firefly Hour**

A moment: the reclaimed hour at dusk — fireflies lighting, the day set down, herself returning.

- Physics: during Firefly Hour she is off-duty from performing competence; the ritual marks the boundary. Door: the 8pm gummy ritual, explicitly the wine-glass replacement.
- Identity-ladder trace: good for the sober ritual-seeker (owns the ritual void) and it plants a flag on *moment ownership* — the census's white space #4 that nobody in the category holds.
- **Why not as the master world:** it's a daypart, and her problem is all-day ("surviving meetings in a fog" is 10am). A moment can't contain the whole 45-day arc or the morning-clarity promise. **Keep it as a room inside the Glow** — Firefly Hour becomes the Glow's evening ritual expression and a named creative territory in Phase 6, not the world itself.

## Value Props (ranked, in customer language)

1. **Herself, back.** — "I feel like myself again — focused, creative, and calm." *(their words and our on-page testimonial, verbatim overlap)* — RTB: the microdose state-shift plus lion's mane/cordyceps/ashwagandha support, on the 45-day arc.
2. **Not a push, not a numb.** — "Coffee pushes a tired brain harder. Wine turns it off for a night. This is the third door — calm *and* clear." — RTB: a state-shift, not stimulation or sedation; supports a steady baseline instead of borrowing against tomorrow.
3. **No mystery blends. Ever.** — "Most smoke-shop gummies are bullshit and don't work" → "a lab report with every batch, real named doses." — RTB: third-party COA every batch, GMP, made in USA. *(This prop is aimed straight at the category's #1 trust wound and at the TRE House cautionary tale.)*
4. **Pre-measured. No guessing. No fear.** — "I've never micro-dosed and I'm scared" → "one small gummy, gentle by design, dosing guide included." — RTB: fixed per-gummy dose, dosing guide + COA in every order, discreet shipping.
5. **Gentle by design — the 45-day return.** — "Week one: lighter mornings. Week three: the snap-at-everyone edge fades. Day 45: she recognizes herself again." — RTB: the four-phase arc; quietly justifies the 3-pouch default (90 gummies ≈ the full arc).

## Voice & Tone

| Attribute | Do | Don't |
|---|---|---|
| **Intimate, not broadcast** (A24 warm) | "Somewhere in the last few years, you went quiet. Let's go find you." | "ATTENTION MOMS! Tired of feeling tired?!" |
| **Dose-honest** (precision as respect) | "One gummy. The same measured dose, every time. The lab report is in the pouch." | "Our proprietary superblend works miracles fast!" |
| **Reverent about the struggle, light about the ritual** | "You've carried everyone. This one small flame is yours." | "Mom fuel! Because adulting is hard, lol." |
| **Enchanted but grounded** (magic as metaphor, never as claim) | "It's not magic. It just remembers where you were." | "Unlock mystical transformation and manifest your best self." |
| **Unhurried confidence** (arc, not urgency) | "It builds. Day 45 tells the story — and you have 60 to decide." | "⚡ ONLY 3 LEFT! 60% OFF ENDS TONIGHT!" |

**Premium guardrail (the line playfulness never crosses):** Playfulness only ever appears as *her recovered lightness* — singing in the car, jokes landing at dinner. The brand never winks at her pain, never gets cute in claims/COA/guarantee contexts, and never touches stoner culture. The magic is atmosphere and metaphor; the promise is always concrete (doses, arc, lab report). If a line could be read as either mocking her or medicating her, it dies.

**Claims floor (hard rules, inherited from brief):** "supports" language only; no disease/medical claims; actives stay general ("microdose gummies") in ad copy; FTC-clean proof (only Jordan T. / Maya S. until real numbers exist); banned words: wellness, journey, holistic.

**Offer-honesty note (from VoC mining, 2026-07-08):** the category's loudest trust wounds after mystery blends are subscription traps ("impossible to cancel" — verbatim on Grüns AND Auri) and "felt nothing" disappointment. Two platform-level responses: (1) no forced autoship — pouch tiers, cancel-anytime stated plainly, the 60-day guarantee kept "no hoops" in practice; (2) the 45-day arc does expectation-setting work in every funnel ("week one is subtle by design") so gentleness reads as design, not weakness. Both are voice-consistent (unhurried confidence) and free differentiators.
