## The five category-level complaints ("five horsemen")

1. **Efficacy doubt / "felt nothing" — the #1 kill-shot.** "You feel nothing. Nothing at all except a minor stomachache. Awful Awful product." (TRE House, Trustpilot via greatcbdshop) · "After taking Auri Mushroom Gummies for 30 days... I didn't feel much of anything." (revgear) · "Good flavor but I didn't really feel much effect... I expected a stronger buzz for the price." (BRĒZ, secondhand) · Goli buyers "question whether benefits are placebo" (garagegymreviews).
2. **Underdosing + mystery blends.** "The mushrooms are probably underdosed... I suspect Auri skimped on the active ingredients" (revgear — cites Wild Lion's Mane 10:1 at just 25mg; 120mg proprietary "Focus Blend") · Goli: "one gummy does not equal the recommended milligrams" (Amazon via honestbrandreviews) · TRE House = the extreme: CDPH warning, unlabeled Schedule I psilocin analogues. **Transparency-per-mg is unclaimed territory.**
3. **Subscription traps.** "They make it impossible to cancel a subscription" (Grüns, PissedConsumer 1.9/5) ≈ "They make it extremely difficult to cancel the subscription." (Auri, Trustpilot) · "They charged me $175.98 for a subscription I never agreed to!" (Grüns) · "I keep trying to cancel the autoship... and they keep sending them." (Auri). **"Actually easy to cancel" is a credible trust differentiator.**
4. **Taste/texture failures.** "My gummies melted" (Grüns) · "stuck together and damp" / "coagulated and got really gooey and goopy" (Goli) · bitterness (TRE House, BRĒZ).
5. **Sugar guilt.** "Gummies or sugar no matter how you look at it" (Grüns) · "Not happy that they contain sugar" (Goli).

## Per-brand essentials

- **Grüns (HIGH):** praise = taste/convenience/energy ("Totally increased my energy, my focus, even my sleep!"). Complaint barbell: Trustpilot ~74% five-star vs ~21% one-star — taste-lovers vs subscription-trap victims ("crooked company," "$244 for another shipment they did not want," "NO HUMAN PHONE CONTACT"). Offer: $79.99/$59.99 sub, 30-day guarantee with $5 return fee + return-doesn't-cancel-sub gotcha (innerbody).
- **Goli (MED-HIGH):** mass-retail value buyer; "These are delicious and taste like candy" · "very easy to eat a whole handful in one sitting" (dosing-control red flag) · "Seen no positive side effects" · weight gain/constipation complaints · ~$15–19/bottle, 53K Amazon reviews on ashwagandha.
- **Auri (HIGH):** praise = fog-lift + taste + habit stickiness ("first supplement I've consistently taken for more than a month"). Complaints = cancel-trap + fulfillment silence ("Since December 26... no response at all. Complete silence!!") + underdosing critique. ~$32–40/bag, 60-day guarantee, Eurofins tested.
- **BRĒZ drinkbrez (MED):** ~4.9/7,000+ site reviews claimed; innerbody: "fast, mellow lift and better sleep without hangovers, though taste polarizes and effects vary by batch"; Amazon split 44% five-star / 24% one-star.

## Market diagnosis (Schwartz)

Solution-aware → product-aware; **sophistication Stage 3–4** — "calm," "focus," "mood" claims are exhausted and no longer differentiate. White space: **mechanism + radical dose transparency** ("here's the exact mg, here's why 25mg lion's mane can't work") + **offer honesty** (easy cancel, real guarantee) + the CDPH scare as the named enemy in a clean-vs-sketchy frame.
