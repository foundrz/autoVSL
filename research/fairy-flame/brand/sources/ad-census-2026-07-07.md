## Category codes

**Visual — legitimacy (keep):** candy-bright product photography ("tastes good" shown not said); UGC phone-shot ad aesthetic; lab-tested badges/QR-to-COA trust cues.
**Visual — open/sameness:** daily-pack pouch ritual (Grüns-only); NO true ad mascot anywhere; scaled leaders all avoid the trippy "shroomy" aesthetic (it's a design-contest cliché, not a leader code).
**Verbal — legitimacy (keep):** "supports/helps" hedge; replacement framing vs. an incumbent habit (melatonin/coffee/alcohol/powders); guarantee-as-copy.
**Verbal — sameness (break candidates):** taste-vs-efficacy paradox line ("works like a supplement, doesn't taste like dirt"); urgency/discount-stack voice (Goli pole).
**Differentiator of the psychoactive wing:** dose-precision honesty (BRĒZ "5–15 minutes, lasting ~1.5 hours"; Diet Smoke "Just Right") — reads as consumer respect.

**Psychoactive-adjacency spectrum:** runs-from-it (Auri strongest, Grüns, Goli, MUD\WTR) · winks-controlled (BRĒZ, Diet Smoke) · winks-hard/crossed (TRE House — CDPH warning NR25-024: products contained Schedule I psilocin analogues; the category's cautionary tale).

## White space (nobody owns)

1. A true ad-character/mascot for mood (loudest open lane, Raindrop-school economics).
2. The calm mid-spectrum: calm as identity/ritual for high-functioning adults — BRĒZ's dose-honest precision voice applied to a non-THC formula. Auri sells calm as clinical utility; BRĒZ/Diet Smoke sell it as legal buzz; the middle is empty.
3. Founder-led creative (five of six brands are faceless; BRĒZ's numbers show the face is a moat).
4. Moment ownership (3pm spiral, Sunday scaries) — all documented hooks are outcome or replacement hooks; nobody owns occasions the way alcohol brands do.
5. Premium botanical-mystic visual world at gummy price points (Moon Juice codes, gummy price) — unclaimed in this category.
