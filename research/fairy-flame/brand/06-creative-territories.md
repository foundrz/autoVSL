## Territory 1: "The Old Photos"

- **Hook mechanic:** a real photograph + an identity confession — "I found this photo from before the kids. I don't recognize her." Photos are infinitely variable (wedding, first job, road trip), so the format regenerates endlessly.
- **Scene in the Glow:** the before-state — her light, visible in an old photo, currently out. The ad is the moment she decides to go get it back.
- **Why the avatar shares it:** it says "this is all of us" without admitting "this is me" — the lowest-friction identity confession in the dataset (her exact words, 274–509-pt threads).
- **15-second sketch:** [0–3s] hands holding a printed photo, dark kitchen / "I don't recognize her." → [3–8s] "I did everything right. Therapy. Clean eating. I'm still not her." → [8–13s] pouch enters frame, ember glow / "Then I found the gentle thing the women in my group kept whispering about." → [13–15s] her face, warm light coming up / "Day 45. She's back."
- **Degradation:** video → static (photo diptych: then / now-with-glow-edge) → email subject: "the photo I couldn't look at"
- **Validated by:** script 01 (photo-before-kids, approved) + script 06 (photo-ten-years-ago).

## Territory 2: "The Witness"

- **Hook mechanic:** the comeback seen through the family's eyes — "My husband asked what changed." / a kid's voice: "Mom laughs again." The buyer isn't the narrator; her glow is.
- **Scene in the Glow:** the Glow's defining physics — everyone can see it before she says a word. This is the witness structure (the dataset's highest-engagement pattern: "My wife thinks I'm funny again," 440 pts) made into a format.
- **Why the avatar shares it:** it's the promise she most wants to overhear — proof the return is visible to the people watching her fade. Sharing = "I want someone to say this about me."
- **15-second sketch:** [0–3s] husband POV, phone-shot / "Something's different about my wife and I finally asked." → [3–9s] "For two years she was… dimmed. Snapping, tired, not her. We both knew." → [9–13s] "Six weeks ago she started these. Last night she was singing while she cooked." → [13–15s] her in warm light, unaware she's watched / "She's back. Ask her what changed."
- **Degradation:** video → static (text-message screenshot: "what's gotten into you lately 😊" / "I'll tell you later") → email subject: "he noticed before I did"
- **Validated by:** script 03 (husband-asked-what-changed, approved).

## Territory 3: "Firefly Hour"

- **Hook mechanic:** the 8pm ritual swap — the wine glass set down, something small and glowing picked up. Owns a *moment* (census white space: nobody in the category owns an occasion the way alcohol brands do).
- **Scene in the Glow:** the door itself — the nightly flame ritual as the entrance to the Glow. Dusk light, day set down, herself returning.
- **Why the avatar shares it:** ritual content is aspirational-performable — "this is my new 8pm" is an identity post. For the sober ritual-seeker it's literally her language: "replace the liquid in the glass but maintain the ritual."
- **15-second sketch:** [0–3s] wine pour begins, stops mid-pour / "Wine isn't calming you. It's borrowing tomorrow's calm." → [3–8s] "Coffee pushes. Wine numbs. I wanted the thing that gives *me* back." → [8–13s] the ritual: pouch-rip, one ember-lit gummy, exhale, dusk window / "One small flame. That's the whole ritual." → [13–15s] "Firefly Hour. Come find yourself."
- **Degradation:** video → static (two glasses: wine glass empty/dark vs. small glowing gummy on a dish, "borrowed calm / your calm") → email subject: "what I do at 8pm now"
- **Validated by:** script 05 (wine-isnt-calming-you, approved); extends it from angle to ownable branded moment.

## Territory 4: "The Third Door"

- **Hook mechanic:** the mechanism-discovery contrarian open — "Everything you've been offered either pushes you or numbs you. There's a third door." Level-2 audiences meet the category AS a discovery (their diagnosed entry point).
- **Scene in the Glow:** the physics lesson — why coffee and wine can't relight her, and what does. The Glow explained without being named as a claim.
- **Why the avatar shares it:** it validates her failed-solution history ("I'm doing everything right and I'm still broken") — sharing says "it was never my fault, the tools were wrong."
- **15-second sketch:** [0–3s] espresso shot + wine pour, split screen / "One pushes your tired brain harder. One turns it off till morning." → [3–9s] "Neither brings you back. That's why you've tried everything and still don't feel like you." → [9–13s] "This is a third thing. A microdose — pre-measured, lab-checked, gentle by design." → [13–15s] arc strip animates days 1→45 / "It builds. 60 days to decide."
- **Degradation:** video → static (three doors graphic: PUSH / NUMB / ⟡ glow-lit third door) → email subject: "coffee, wine, and the third door"
- **Validated by:** scripts 02/09 (red-flags + stop-coffee-for-fog) share its mechanism spine.

## Territory 5: "The Lab Report"

- **Hook mechanic:** trust-expose — "I opened the lab report on gas-station mushroom gummies. Then I opened ours." The category's scam wound (state-warned smoke-shop gummies, "most are bullshit and don't work") is the enemy; the COA is the hero device.
- **Scene in the Glow:** the Glow's floor — nothing glows that isn't verified. Zone-C energy: zero whimsy, maximum specificity. Glim never appears here (mascot exclusion rule).
- **Why the avatar shares it:** protective sharing — "before you buy ANY of these, watch this" makes her the informed guardian of her group chat (her advice-network role in the avatar research).
- **15-second sketch:** [0–3s] gas-station gummy pack tossed on table / "California told people to stop eating these. Want to know what was inside?" → [3–8s] "Mystery compounds. No doses on the label. That's the category I sell in. It makes me furious." (founder voice) → [8–13s] COA unfolds on screen, real mg circled / "So every batch we make ships with this. Every. Batch." → [13–15s] "Real doses. A lab report in your pouch. That's the whole pitch."
- **Degradation:** video → static (two labels side by side: "Proprietary Blend™" redacted vs. our named-mg panel) → email subject: "open the lab report before you swallow anything"
- **Validated by:** script 10 (gas-station-gummies expose, approved); founder-voice variant doubles as brand-trust content.

## Channel Sequencing

### Stage 1 — DTC VSL funnel + email (NOW)
- **Cold paid (Meta/TikTok):** launch Territories 1 + 4 (Old Photos for emotion-led, Third Door for mechanism-led) — one core idea per campaign; both speak Level-2 discovery. Territory 5 runs as the trust flanker for comparison-shopper audiences (Level 3–4).
- **Retargeting:** Territory 2 (The Witness) — the emotional closer for warm traffic; Territory 3 (Firefly Hour) against sober-curious and evening-scroller segments.
- **What proves the platform converts:** hook-level CTR by territory (bank feedback loop), 3-pouch take-rate (arc framing working), day-45→repurchase (Glow retention). Per structure: each VSL script → 4 edits × 4 hooks, all citing bank ids.
- **Email machine (the arc IS the flow):** Day 0 welcome ("your first flame" + dosing guide + COA), Day 7 ("week one is subtle by design" — expectation-set against the felt-nothing kill-shot), Day 21 (baseline check-in + replenish), Day 45 ("she's back" story capture — testimonial harvest), Day 60 (Glow invite / cross-mood sampler, per existing upsell path). Abandoned-cart runs Territory 5 proof, not discounts (voice rule: no urgency-stack).
- **Spokesperson program:** founder voice on Territories 4/5 (his story earns the fury and the trust); women 45–55 avatar-matched spokespeople on 1/2/3 (per offer doc: tested per avatar).

### Stage 2 — Retail readiness (MUCH LATER — checklist only, no action now)
